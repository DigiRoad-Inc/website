The Problem

The world is evolving at a pace unlike anything in recent history. Financial systems have moved to real-time rails. Identity has become digital and borderless. Data flows across institutions and continents in milliseconds.

Yet universities , the very institutions charged with preparing people for this world , remain fragmented, operating on disconnected systems built for a different era. Student records, payment infrastructure, and communication tools are siloed, legacy-driven, and painfully manual.

This is not a technology problem unique to a single institution. It is a structural problem that has compounded quietly over decades. University IT infrastructure has not kept pace with the demands placed on it , and the cost is felt by every administrator, every student, and every institution trying to compete on a global stage.

Despite this, universities remain among the most important institutions in the world. They drive innovation, create talent pipelines, and generate enormous economic and cultural value. The gap between their influence and their infrastructure has never been wider.

"The institutions shaping the future are operating on systems from the past."

Education is the foundation of every economy. It drives innovation, produces the talent that builds companies and governments, and generates enormous economic activity in every region of the world. Yet it remains one of the most under-digitised sectors in existence.

Most technology companies have overlooked higher education , treating it as too complex, too fragmented, and too resistant to change to be worth building for. The result: a $6.5 trillion sector operating on infrastructure that would be unacceptable in any other industry of comparable scale.

We see it differently. We see a sector that is ready for modern infrastructure. One that wants to evolve, has the financial scale to support real investment, and whose evolution will compound outward , improving outcomes for hundreds of millions of students, families, and economies.
